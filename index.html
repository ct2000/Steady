Assumption checker

Challenge “must”, “should”, “always”, and “never”.

 ⌄

Pick the rule that might be running

Three-step check

  1. Name the rule: “The rule I’m holding is…”
  2. Separate impact from intent: “This affected me, but do I know they meant harm?”
  3. Choose a softer assumption: “There may be another explanation.”

Flexible assumptions reduce anxiety, resentment, and unnecessary conflict.

Guided journeys

Choose support based on what is happening right now.

 ⌄

I’m angry right now

  • Rate your anger first.
  • Slow your breathing.
  • Leave the conversation if needed.
  • Do not text/react at 8+/10.

I’m ruminating

  • Name the loop.
  • Challenge mind-reading.
  • Move your body for 10 minutes.
  • Refocus on facts, not imagined intent.

I need to reply calmly

  • Use “I feel…” language.
  • Keep messages short.
  • Separate impact from intent.
  • Ask before assuming.

I want to understand the trigger

  • What rule got activated?
  • What vulnerable feeling sits underneath?
  • What need wasn’t expressed?
  • What would calm-me say?

Safety and support

Use extra help if anger feels unsafe or destructive.

 ⌄

When to step away

  • You feel close to shouting, threatening, breaking things, or violence.
  • You are under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • The other person is escalating or unsafe.
  • You cannot think clearly enough to be fair.

UK urgent support

If there is immediate danger, call 999.

If you feel at risk of harming yourself or someone else, contact urgent local support, NHS 111, your GP, or a crisis line.

This toolkit is self-help, not a substitute for professional care.
